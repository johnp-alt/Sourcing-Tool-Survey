Key findings
📈
LinkedIn is essential — but response rates are the #1 pain point
5 of 6 recruiters flagged low response rates as the primary reason to add a new tool. This is consistent across India and Singapore sourcing.
🎯
Instahyre is the clear team consensus
5 of 6 team members recommended Instahyre. Multiple have used it before, Aarthi has an existing proposal negotiated, and Farheen has direct contacts there — meaning we can move fast.
💻
GitHub Jobs is the #2 pick — especially for SWE roles
3 team members recommended GitHub Jobs. Aarthi noted that a large portion of SWE candidates are active GitHub users, making it a high-intent channel for technical talent.
🌏
Singapore-specific tools are an underserved gap
NodeFlair and Tech in Asia Jobs were raised for Singapore sourcing specifically — neither is currently in use. Worth exploring alongside Instahyre for the SG market.
💡
Team has prior experience — reducing ramp-up risk
Aaron has been part of pilots for JuiceBox, Seekout and Weekday. Madhur has used JuiceBox.ai at a previous org. Farheen used Instahyre at Google. This institutional knowledge de-risks any new tool rollout.

Our Recommendation

The team unanimously agrees: LinkedIn Recruiter is a strong, high-value platform we should continue using. The ask is simple — add one complementary tool to address the response rate gap and expand our reach for niche tech talent in India and Singapore.

Based on team consensus, prior experience, and existing vendor relationships, we recommend starting with a pilot of Instahyre. Aarthi already has a negotiated proposal ready to revisit, and Farheen can facilitate a demo. For Singapore specifically, NodeFlair and Tech in Asia Jobs are worth a secondary evaluation.
